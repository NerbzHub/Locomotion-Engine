# Locomotion Engine
## Book 00 — Vision

## Section 1 — Purpose

The Locomotion Engine exists to provide a stable, reusable foundation
for creating games. It is developed as an engine first, with individual
games serving as applications of its capabilities rather than defining
its identity. By separating enduring engine responsibilities from
game-specific content, the project seeks to establish a platform that
can support both present and future creative work.

The immediate purpose of the engine is to enable the development of a
whimsical procedural pixel art game. This game serves as the first
practical expression of the engine and provides a continuous source of
real-world requirements that shape its evolution. As the engine matures,
it should remain capable of supporting additional games and experiences
without becoming coupled to the needs of any single project.

The engine is created to encourage deliberate, sustainable development.
It provides a shared foundation that reduces repeated effort, promotes
consistency, and allows improvements made for one game to strengthen the
capabilities available to future games. Each advancement to the engine
is intended to increase the value of the platform as a whole rather than
solve isolated problems in temporary ways.

This purpose establishes the enduring direction of the project. Every
architectural decision, implementation effort, and contribution should
ultimately strengthen the engine's ability to enable the creation of
games, beginning with its first whimsical procedural pixel art game
while preserving the flexibility to support future creative ambitions.

## Section 2 — Goals

The goals of the Locomotion Engine define what the project is intended
to achieve over its lifetime. They provide enduring direction for the
evolution of the engine without prescribing how those outcomes are to be
realized.

The immediate goal of the project is to produce a working, browser-first
game engine capable of creating a complete whimsical procedural pixel
art game. Delivering a finished game demonstrates that the engine is
capable of supporting a real production experience and provides a
practical foundation for its continued development.

Beyond its first game, the engine is intended to become a reusable
platform for future projects. Knowledge, capabilities, and improvements
gained during the development of the initial game should strengthen the
engine itself so that subsequent games can be created more efficiently
while benefiting from an increasingly mature foundation.

Over the long term, the project seeks to establish a stable,
maintainable, and extensible engine whose value extends beyond any
individual title. The engine should remain capable of supporting new
ideas, new genres, and evolving requirements without being defined by
the needs of its first implementation alone.

These goals are enduring objectives rather than temporary milestones.
Individual features, technologies, tools, and development plans may
change as the project evolves, but each should contribute toward
creating a practical, reusable engine that enables the development of
games over the long term.

## Section 3 — Scope

The Locomotion Engine project is the development of a reusable
browser-first 2D game engine together with the work necessary to
demonstrate, validate, and evolve that engine through the creation of
complete games. The engine is the primary product of the project.
Individual games exist to exercise its capabilities, confirm its
architectural decisions, and provide practical evidence of its
effectiveness.

The initial demonstration of the engine is a whimsical procedural pixel
art game. While this game establishes the project's first destination,
it does not define the engine's lifetime. The engine is intended to
support additional games, experiments, and future projects without being
fundamentally tied to the requirements, mechanics, or content of any
single title.

The scope of the project includes the capabilities required to build,
maintain, and evolve reusable 2D browser-based games. Features are
considered in scope when they strengthen the engine as a general-purpose
foundation or directly support the development and validation of games
built upon it.

The project does not seek to become a universal game engine or to
address every possible game development domain. Support for unrelated
platforms, genres, technologies, or specialised capabilities is outside
the scope of the project unless they become necessary to fulfil the
engine's intended purpose. Likewise, functionality that serves only a
single game without improving the engine as a reusable foundation should
not be incorporated into the engine itself.

These boundaries ensure that development remains focused on creating a
durable engine whose value extends beyond its first game, while avoiding
unnecessary expansion that increases complexity without advancing the
project's intended scope.

## Section 4 — Design Philosophy

The Locomotion Engine is intended to be a practical engineering project
before it is an academic exercise. Its value is measured by the quality
of the software it enables rather than the volume of documentation it
produces. Every design decision should therefore support the steady
creation of a capable, maintainable engine that can evolve through real
use.

The preferred approach is to build working software early, learn from
implementation, and refine the architecture through experience.
Practical understanding gained from developing and using the engine
provides a stronger foundation for long-term decisions than speculation
about hypothetical future requirements. Progress through implementation
should remain the primary source of architectural insight.

Simplicity is preferred wherever it can satisfy the problem effectively.
Unnecessary complexity increases the cost of development, maintenance,
and future evolution without providing lasting value. Designs should
remain approachable, understandable, and easy to extend so that the
engine continues to be practical to develop over many years.

The engine should also be resilient to change. New knowledge, better
techniques, and evolving requirements are expected throughout its
lifetime. The design philosophy therefore encourages steady improvement
through measured refinement rather than large-scale reinvention.
Evolution should be guided by demonstrated experience, preserving what
has proven valuable while improving what has not.

Documentation exists to support this process, not to replace it. Its
purpose is to capture enduring understanding, communicate architectural
intent, and reduce future uncertainty. It should remain concise,
relevant, and closely aligned with the reality of the engine as it
exists, allowing implementation and documentation to strengthen one
another over time.

The long-term ambition is not to construct the most elaborate engine
possible, but to build one that remains understandable, reliable,
enjoyable to develop, and capable of supporting meaningful games for
many years to come.

## Section 5 — Success Criteria

The Locomotion Engine succeeds when it consistently enables the creation
of high-quality games over an extended period of time. Success is
measured not by the quantity of documentation produced, the
sophistication of individual technologies, or the completion of isolated
features, but by the engine's continued ability to support the design,
development, and evolution of enjoyable, well-crafted games.

The creation of the first whimsical procedural pixel art game represents
the project's first significant demonstration of success. It validates
that the engine fulfills its intended purpose and provides practical
evidence that its architectural direction is sound. This milestone marks
the beginning of the engine's useful life rather than its final
objective, establishing a foundation upon which future games and
capabilities can be built.

A successful engine remains understandable, maintainable, and adaptable
as it evolves. It accommodates new ideas without unnecessary disruption,
supports extension without compromising existing foundations, and
remains approachable for future development. Growth should strengthen
the engine rather than increase its complexity without corresponding
value.

Success is also reflected in the longevity of the architecture.
Decisions made today should continue to provide value as the engine
matures, allowing knowledge, systems, and experience to accumulate
instead of requiring continual reinvention. The engine should remain
useful across multiple projects, changing requirements, and future
iterations while preserving a coherent architectural identity.

The project's documentation succeeds when it continues to support
implementation by communicating enduring intent, architectural
understanding, and design rationale. Documentation is valuable only
insofar as it helps developers build, maintain, and evolve the engine.
It is a means of enabling successful implementation rather than an
objective in its own right.

Ultimately, the Locomotion Engine is successful when it becomes a
dependable foundation for creating games, demonstrating that its
architecture remains practical, sustainable, and capable of supporting
creative work long after its initial implementation.
